Polaris - Our New Catalog

Our new online catalog system is now here. To access information about your account, you can now log on through the front page of our website. Please note that the new catalog system requires the use of a password. Passwords are the same as your pin number.

Do-It-Yourself Craft

Thursday, December 20, 3:00pm-5:00pm

Youths under 8 are welcome to stop between 3pm-5pm to create a holiday craft. This is a Do-It-Yourself craft to be completed with both parent/guardian and child.

Youth Movie: The Lorax

Wednesday, December 26, 7:00pm

Join us at the library for a special viewing of the Lorax, a PG rated animated fiml about a 12-year-old boy searches for the one thing that will enable him to win the affection of the girl of his dreams. To find it he must discover the story of the Lorax, the grumpy yet charming creature who fights to protect his world.

Book Bingo For Youths ages 5-10

Thursday, December 27, 11:00am

Youths, ages 5-10, are invited to play the 'literary version' of bingo on 12/27 at 11am. Instead of numbered cards, this game will feature favorite children authors, book characters, and book titles from classic to newly popular. No registration is required.

Tuesday Movies Announcement

As FEMA will be using our facilities, our Tuesday afternoon movie events on December 11th and 18th to be shown at 1 pm at the Secaucus Senior Center, 101 Centre Avenue.

Youth Art Months: November through December

Youths in grades Pre-K to 12th are invited to display their art at the library during the months of November and December. All types of art are welcome, including painting, drawings, sculptures, crafts, photography, crafts, etc. All pictures and photographs must be framed or mounted when brought to the library. The name, grade, and phone number of the child must be on the back of each submission.

New Museum Passes

Did you know that the library offers several museum passes to library card users for free? To find out more information about participating museums, visit our page about museums. We are pleased to annouced two new museums Morris Museum and the Children's Museum of Manhattan.

HeritageQuest Online

Users can access HeritageQuest Online at home with their library card. HeritageQuest is a comprehensive treasury of American genealogical sources. Please use the following link to access this database from your own home computer: http://tinyurl.com/d4epdfl

Ongoing Book Sale

Stop by the library during our business hours to see which books we have on sale. All books are $1.00 each regardless of size or publication date. The books on sale are located on the bookcase across from the community bulletin board. All proceeds benefit the library's collection of new materials.

Ebooks Available Through eBCCLS

As of 3/12, BCCLS has launched a special collection just for BCCLS patrons, and can be accessed by visiting: bccls.org/ebccls.shtml. By clicking on this link you can also access the database, Naxos Music Library and Spoke Word Library.
